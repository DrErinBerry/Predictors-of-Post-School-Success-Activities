‚úÖ SMART Goal Framework Reference

Use this reference guide when teaching students about SMART goals or when evaluating goal quality. This page can be printed and displayed as a poster or given to students as a handout.

S
SPECIFIC
The goal clearly states WHAT is to be achieved, by WHOM, WHERE, and WHEN.
‚ùå Weak: "I want to get better at math."
‚úÖ Strong: "By December 2025, I will improve my algebra grade from a C to a B by attending tutoring twice a week."
M
MEASURABLE
There is a clear way to determine IF and WHEN the goal has been achieved.
‚ùå Weak: "I want to save money."
‚úÖ Strong: "I will save $500 by putting $50 from each paycheck into my savings account."
A
ACHIEVABLE
The goal is realistic given the student's resources, skills, support, and timeframe.
‚ùå Weak: "I will become a licensed physician by next month."
‚úÖ Strong: "I will complete my CNA certification program within 6 months while working part-time."
R
RELEVANT
The goal aligns with the student's long-term vision and post-school outcomes.
‚ùå Weak: "I will learn to juggle." (when student wants to be an accountant)
‚úÖ Strong: "I will complete a basic accounting course to prepare for my career goal."
T
TIME-BOUND
The goal includes a specific deadline or timeframe for completion.
‚ùå Weak: "Someday I'll get my driver's license."
‚úÖ Strong: "I will earn my driver's license by my 18th birthday in March 2026."

üí° Teaching Tip:

A good SMART goal answers these questions:

  • What exactly will be accomplished?
  • How will I know when I've achieved it?
  • Is it possible with my current resources and support?
  • Why does this goal matter to my future?
  • When will I complete this goal?

üí° Implementation Tips

Start with Student Interests: Goals are more meaningful when they connect to things students care about

Use Visual Supports: Create visual representations of goals to make abstract concepts concrete

Model the Process: Share your own goals and goal-setting process with students

Celebrate Small Wins: Break big goals into tiny steps and celebrate each accomplishment

Make it Real: Connect goal-setting to real-world activities for authentic contexts

Connect to IEPs: Help students see the link between personal goals and IEP transition goals
