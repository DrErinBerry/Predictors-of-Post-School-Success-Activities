Applying the "Fit" Framework to Student Scenarios

Understanding the "Fit" Framework

Before analyzing our case studies, remember the three key considerations for matching predictors to individual students:

üßë‚Äçüéì1. The Person's Profile as a Learner

  • How does the student prefer to learn new information or skills?
  • How do they best apply knowledge and skills to tasks?
  • What are their strengths and challenges?

üéØ2. The Purpose

  • Is the student learning something new?
  • Are they applying existing skills to a new situation?
  • Are they looking to improve performance in a specific area?

üåç3. The Environment or Context

  • Where will the learning/skill application take place?
  • Is it in a classroom, workplace, home, or community setting?
  • Are they participating in academic, vocational, or recreational activities?

üìöCase Study: Maria's Transition Journey

Student Profile

Maria is a 16-year-old student with autism spectrum disorder who excels in mathematics and pattern recognition. She has strong visual learning skills but struggles with verbal instructions and social interactions. Maria becomes anxious in noisy, unpredictable environments but thrives with routine and clear expectations. She's expressed interest in working with computers and data but has limited work experience.

Current Situation

Maria's IEP team is planning her transition services for the next two years. They need to select appropriate predictors that will help her achieve her post-school goal of employment in the technology field while living semi-independently.

ü§îAnalysis Activity

Part 1: Analyze Maria's "Fit" Profile

Based on the case study, identify:

A. Learner Profile:

  • Visual learner (strength)
  • Struggles with verbal instructions
  • Needs routine and structure
  • Pattern recognition skills
  • Limited social comfort

B. Purpose (What Maria needs):

  • Build employment skills for tech field
  • Develop independent living skills
  • Improve social/communication skills for workplace
  • Gain real work experience

C. Environment/Context Considerations:

  • Needs quiet, structured settings initially
  • May need gradual exposure to workplace environments
  • Home and community practice for living skills
  • Technology-rich learning environments

Part 2: Match Predictors to Maria's Needs

Instructions: For each predictor category below, determine if it would be a good "fit" for Maria and explain why using the three considerations.

1. Paid Work Experience

Consider: Would a paid internship at a local tech company be appropriate? What modifications might be needed?
Good fit with modifications
  • Learner Profile Match: Aligns with her tech interests and visual/pattern strengths
  • Purpose Match: Provides real employment experience in her field of interest
  • Environment Needs: Would need to start with a quiet, structured workplace (maybe back-office data entry) before moving to busier environments. Consider part-time hours initially.

2. Social Skills Training

Consider: What type of social skills instruction would match Maria's learning style?
Excellent fit with modifications
  • Learner Profile Match: Must use visual supports (social stories, video modeling) rather than verbal instruction alone
  • Purpose Match: Critical for workplace success and semi-independent living
  • Environment Needs: Start in small group or 1:1 settings, practice in controlled environments before generalizing to community

3. Community Experiences

Consider: How could community experiences be structured for Maria's success?
Good fit with modifications
  • Learner Profile Match: Provide visual schedules and preparation materials before outings
  • Purpose Match: Essential for independent living goals
  • Environment Needs: Start with quieter community settings (library, small stores during off-peak hours) and gradually increase complexity

4. Self-Determination/Self-Advocacy

Consider: How can self-advocacy instruction accommodate Maria's communication style?
Excellent fit with modifications
  • Learner Profile Match: Use visual supports, written scripts, and technology-assisted communication options
  • Purpose Match: Critical for requesting workplace accommodations and living semi-independently
  • Environment Needs: Practice in safe settings first, potentially use email/text for advocacy when verbal is challenging

Part 3: Your Recommendations

Based on your analysis, answer these questions:

1. Which TWO predictors would you prioritize for Maria in Year 1? Why?

2. What modifications would be essential for Maria's success with these predictors?

3. How would you sequence these predictors across her remaining high school years?

üìùKey Takeaway

Remember: Predictors are only as effective as the match between the intervention and the individual student's needs. The same predictor implemented the same way will not work for every student. Always consider:

  • How the student learns best
  • What they're trying to achieve
  • Where the learning will happen

The "fit" framework ensures we're not just implementing predictors, but implementing the RIGHT predictors in the RIGHT way for each individual student.
